## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### Day 1 — Oct 16 (Fri) · Fly in + Night drive to Shillong
Fly Chennai 14:40 → Guwahati 17:45. Grab dinner near the airport, then night drive to **Shillong** (~3h, arrive ~21:30). Check in and crash early — big day tomorrow.
**Travel:** 3h flight + 3h drive.
**Overnight: Shillong.**

### Day 2 — Oct 17 (Sat) · Sunrise Laitlum + Nongriat Trek
Wake early, drive to **Laitlum Canyon** (~45 km from Shillong, "End of Hills" — 360° views of endless valleys with clouds below). Sunrise/early morning light with mist in the valleys — arguably better than afternoon. Spend ~1.5h. Then drive to **Tyrna village** (~2.5h from Laitlum). Trek down ~3,500 steps through jungle to **Nongriat village**. Visit the **Double Decker Living Root Bridge** (the only one in the world) and **Rainbow Falls** (crystal-clear pool with rainbows in the mist — skip the first lagoon, the blue lagoon later is better). **Sleep overnight in Nongriat village.**
**Travel:** 1h drive to Laitlum + 1.5h at canyon + 2.5h drive to Tyrna + 1.5h descent. Arrive Nongriat by ~early afternoon.
**Note:** This is a packed day. Leave Shillong by 5:30 AM to make it work comfortably.
**Overnight: Nongriat village.**

### Day 3 — Oct 18 (Sun) · Trek Up + Shnongpdeng
Early morning trek back up from Nongriat (~2.5h, 3,500 steps). Drive to **Shnongpdeng** village on the Umngot River.
**Camp overnight by the river.**
**Travel:** 2.5h climb + ~3h drive.
**Overnight: Shnongpdeng (riverside camping, ~₹400/head).**

#### About Shnongpdeng
- **Location:** West Jaintia Hills, Meghalaya. ~90 km from Shillong via Dawki (~3h drive). 2 km from Dawki but far quieter. Near the India-Bangladesh border.
- **How to reach:** Shillong → Dawki road (3h) → take the Shnongpdeng turn before Dawki town. Or via Jowai–Amlarem route.
- **The river:** Umngot River — same crystal-clear water as Dawki but without the tourist circus. Boats appear to float on air. 15m visibility in October.
- **Activities:** Freediving, snorkeling, scuba (₹3,500 if interested), cliff jumping, kayaking, boating.
- **Best time:** Oct–Feb (water is clearest, transparent emerald-green).
- **Why not Dawki:** Dawki main point is overcrowded, noisy, boats lined up one after another. Shnongpdeng is the same river, fraction of the crowd.

### Day 4 — Oct 19 (Mon) · Shnongpdeng → Guwahati → Bomdila (Kalaktang route)
Morning freedive session in the Umngot. Then drive to Guwahati (~3h), pick up the rental car, and drive the **Kalaktang route** to Bomdila (~256 km, ~7h). Passes through **Zangdokpalri Monastery** en route.
**Travel:** 3h drive + 7h ride. **Long day — start early.**
**Overnight: Bomdila.**

### Day 5 — Oct 20 (Tue) · Bomdila → Dirang (acclimatize)
Short drive to **Dirang** (~1.5h). Rest and acclimatize before the high passes. Explore **Sangti Valley** (riverside meadows, orchards), **hot springs**, and **Mandala Top** (108 stupas in concentric circles).
**Travel:** 1.5h drive + local exploring.
**Overnight: Dirang (~1,500 m).**

### Day 6 — Oct 21 (Wed) · Dirang → Tawang via Sela Pass
The highlight drive. Cross **Sela Pass (13,700 ft)** — frozen lake, war memorial, army tea canteen. **Cross before noon** as winds and fog roll in. Stop at **Jaswant Garh War Memorial** and **Nuranang (Jang) Falls** (100m cascade). The 2024 Sela Tunnel keeps the route open even in snow.
**Travel:** ~6h drive (135 km).
**Overnight: Tawang (~3,048 m).**

### Day 7 — Oct 22 (Thu) · Tawang Monastery + Bumla Pass + Lakes
Pre-dawn visit to **Tawang Monastery** for morning prayers (largest monastery in India, before tour groups arrive). Then taxi to **Bumla Pass (~15,200 ft)** — Indo-China border, army briefing. Stop at **PTSO Lake** (travelers say it's more impressive than Madhuri Lake) and **Madhuri Lake (Sangetsar Tso)** on the way.
**Note:** Must travel in a local Arunachal-registered taxi (~₹5,000). No bikes allowed. Permit arranged day before at DC office (booth inside War Memorial campus). Weather-dependent.
**Travel:** Full day, 3-4h taxi round trip.
**Overnight: Tawang.**

### Day 8 — Oct 23 (Fri) · Tawang → Bomdila
Begin the descent. Recross Sela early, slower pace, more photo time. Visit **Bomdila Monastery**.
**Travel:** 6-7h drive.
**Overnight: Bomdila.**

### Day 9 — Oct 24 (Sat) · Bomdila → Guwahati
Long final leg back to the plains. Return the car in Guwahati.
**Travel:** 8-9h drive. Start early, mountain delays are common.
**Overnight: Guwahati.**

### Day 10 — Oct 25 (Sun) · Buffer day in Guwahati
Rest day. Absorbs any delays from the ride. Explore Guwahati if you want, or just recover.
**Overnight: Guwahati.**

### Day 11 — Oct 26 (Mon) · Fly home
Fly Guwahati 18:30 → Chennai 21:30.

## Permits

- **Inner Line Permit (ILP):** Required for all Indian citizens to enter Arunachal Pradesh. Apply online or at facilitation counters in Guwahati (Arunachal Bhavan), Tezpur, or Bhalukpong. Carry printed and digital copies + government photo ID at all times.
- **Bumla Pass permit:** Separate special permit from DC office in Tawang, countersigned by Army. Arrange day before. Foreign nationals not allowed at Bumla.

## Practical Tips

- **Cash:** Load up at Guwahati/Bomdila. ATMs past Bomdila are unreliable. Budget ₹1,500–3,000/day.
- **Connectivity:** BSNL and Airtel work patchily; Jio unreliable. Download offline maps before leaving Shillong.
- **Altitude:** Acclimatize in Dirang (Day 5). Carry Diamox if doctor advises. Full-face helmet, thermals, windproof jacket, waterproofs essential.
- **Start early every day; cross Sela before noon.** Winds and poor visibility set in by midday.
- **Food:** Army-canteen Maggi and tea on high stretches (Sela, Bumla). Carry snacks and water.
- **Medical:** Personal kit with altitude meds, painkillers, prescriptions. Nearest major hospital is a long drive away.

## Experience Priority (what we kept and why)

### Must-do (in the plan)
- **Laitlum Canyon** — best viewpoint in NE India, minimal crowds
- **Nongriat Double Decker Root Bridge + Rainbow Falls** — only double-decker living root bridge in the world. Overnight in village.
- **Shnongpdeng freediving** — India's only river diving spot, 15m visibility, crystal-clear Umngot River
- **Sela Pass** — 13,700 ft, frozen lake, the ride itself is the experience
- **Tawang Monastery at sunrise** — second largest monastery in the world, valley views
- **Bumla Pass + PTSO Lake** — Indo-China border at 15,200 ft
- **Sangti Valley + Dirang hot springs** — genuine acclimatization day with real experiences

### Deliberately dropped
- **Mawsmai Cave** — generic tourist cave (user is claustrophobic anyway)
- **Sacred Forest Mawphlang** — good but not top-tier, created time pressure
- **Dawki** — overcrowded, noisy; replaced with Shnongpdeng (same river, no crowds)
- **Tawang town tourist stops** (craft centre, Giant Buddha, old market) — average
- **Seven Sisters Falls** — often dry in October
- **Kaziranga** — would need 2 extra days
- **Zemithang** — amazing but needs 1-2 extra days we don't have

### Would add with more time
- **Kongthong whistling village** — every person has a unique melody as their name (+half day)
- **Zemithang** — deep Monpa country beyond Tawang (+1-2 days)
- **Krem Mawmluh caving** — India's 4th longest cave system (+half day, but user is claustrophobic)
- **Thembang Monpa homestay** — on the Tawang route, real cultural immersion (+1 night)
